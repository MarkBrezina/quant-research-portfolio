## Crypto Strategy Adaptation

I have also been adapting systematic research frameworks to cryptocurrencies through an asset-agnostic lens. Rather than anchoring analysis to traditional company fundamentals, I treat crypto assets as markets shaped more heavily by investor attention, sentiment, narrative momentum, technological differentiation, and network effects. That means focusing on the drivers that appear most relevant in practice for digital assets: hype cycles, behavioural participation, liquidity shifts, and structural adoption signals, alongside more classical market features such as volatility, cross-asset dependence, and supply-demand imbalance. Research has found that investor attention and sentiment can help explain or predict crypto returns and volatility, while recent work also shows strong predictive content in order flow for crypto returns.

My broader approach is to combine this crypto-specific perspective with a unified market framework that still remains systematic across asset classes. In practice, that means analysing cryptocurrencies through market participant behaviour, order flows, market states, and execution dynamics rather than forcing them into an equity-style valuation template. This fits well with the view that unbacked crypto prices are largely driven by supply and demand, information flows, and interaction effects, while official monitoring frameworks increasingly track both on-chain and off-chain indicators to understand crypto market activity.
