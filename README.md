## Selected research themes

### Short-horizon alpha research and backtesting

My work includes short-horizon to mid-frequency signal research across traditional markets, alongside parallel research and backtesting in crypto. Areas explored include multi-horizon trend signals, cross-sectional residual mean reversion, volatility-aware scaling, and regime-conditioned strategy behaviour.

### Order-book and flow-based market microstructure

A major focus has been on extracting signal from order-book imbalance, order-flow imbalance, liquidity persistence, and short-term equilibrium deviations. This includes research into how imbalance and flow interact with drift, reversal, and tradability at different horizons.

### Regime detection and adaptive behaviour

I have also worked on market-state and regime-oriented research, including clustering, change-point ideas, volatility conditioning, and state-dependent behaviour in signals and portfolio allocation.

### Execution-aware quantitative research

Rather than treating alpha in isolation, I try to evaluate ideas in a more realistic setting by incorporating transaction costs, slippage, spread, participation assumptions, and execution-sensitive decay.

### Crypto market adaptation

In crypto, my work has focused on adapting the same research logic to BTC and selected altcoins, with attention to exchange-based data, liquidity differences, volatility, and market-structure-specific behaviour.
