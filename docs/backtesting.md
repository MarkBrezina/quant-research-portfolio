## Backtesting & Strategy Research

I have built and worked with a modular Python backtesting stack for systematic trading research, covering data handling, reusable strategy logic, execution-style abstractions, and framework adapters. My work spans custom in-house backtesting code as well as integrations around Backtrader, Backtesting.py, and VectorBT, allowing me to test strategies across different research and simulation workflows. These libraries are widely used for Python strategy research: Backtrader is designed as a feature-rich framework for backtesting and trading, Backtesting.py supports running and optimizing parameterized strategies, and VectorBT is built around fast vectorized analysis on pandas/NumPy objects.

The repo structure you shared suggests that this work has not been limited to one-off notebooks. Instead, you have developed a reusable framework with separated modules for strategy adapters, core market/data types, OHLCV handling, runner logic, strategy implementations, and research notebooks, alongside datasets covering both traditional assets and cryptocurrencies. That points to practical experience in testing ideas across equities and crypto rather than only in toy examples. In your broader framework documents, you also describe the goal as producing reproducible code and backtests across asset classes. 

Beyond simply running backtests, your write-up also shows that you have been thinking carefully about validation quality. You describe out-of-sample testing, rolling validation, statistical double-testing, bootstrapping, White’s Reality Check, factor attribution, and performance attribution as part of deciding whether a strategy is robust enough to move forward. You also frame the research stack as supporting scenario analysis, Monte Carlo testing, and portfolio-level what-if analysis, which makes the backtesting work more rigorous and closer to institutional research standards than a basic single-strategy equity curve exercise.
